Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel laureate who is credited with saving over a billion people from starvation. His work in developing high-yielding varieties of wheat and promoting agricultural practices helped to alleviate hunger in many parts of the world.

Borlaug's research and advocacy for agricultural development earned him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970, and he is often referred to as the "Father of the Green Revolution".
